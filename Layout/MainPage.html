Discover the Legacy of Rumah Penghulu Abu Seman

The Rumah Penghulu Abu Seman was built in stages between 1910 and the early 1930s. It was owned by the local headman of Mukim Bagan Samak, Bandar Baharu, Kedah. Originally located in Kampung Sungai Kechil, a small village in the northern part of Peninsula Malaysia, this traditional Malay house stands as a testament to the rich architectural heritage and cultural traditions of the region. Each room tells a story of craftsmanship, community, and the elegant simplicity of traditional Malay living.
